GDC Investigations

Independent guidance for UK dentists facing General Dental Council investigations, fitness to practise proceedings, and regulatory scrutiny.

Understanding GDC Investigations

A General Dental Council (GDC) investigation is a formal regulatory process assessing whether a dentist’s fitness to practise is impaired. These investigations can arise from patient complaints, employer referrals, criminal matters, or regulatory concerns.

The GDC Fitness to Practise Process

Initial Assessment

The GDC first assesses whether a concern meets the threshold for further action. Many cases are closed at this early stage.

Case Examiners

If progressed, case examiners review evidence and decide whether to take no action, issue warnings, agree undertakings, or refer the case to a hearing.

Fitness to Practise Hearings

Serious cases may proceed to a public hearing, where sanctions can include conditions, suspension, or erasure from the dental register.

Common Triggers for GDC Investigations

  • Dental negligence allegations
  • Poor clinical records or consent issues
  • Dishonesty or probity concerns
  • Criminal convictions or cautions
  • Health concerns affecting performance

Responding to a GDC Investigation

Prompt, professional responses are essential. Dentists are strongly advised to seek guidance from dental defence organisations or specialist GDC solicitors.

Impact on Dentists

GDC investigations can cause stress, financial pressure, and reputational harm. Understanding the process and preparing appropriately can significantly reduce risk.
