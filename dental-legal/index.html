Dental Legal Advice for Dentists & Practices (UK)

Welcome to the UK dental legal hub. This section provides authoritative guidance on dental negligence claims, GDC investigations, dental indemnity insurance, employment law, contracts, regulatory compliance and legal risk management for dentists, dental practices and dental businesses.

Dental Negligence & Clinical Claims

Dental negligence claims are one of the highest-risk and highest-cost legal issues facing UK dentists. Patients may bring claims for misdiagnosis, poor treatment outcomes, failed implants, cosmetic dentistry errors, endodontic failures, orthodontic issues and consent-related disputes.

Understanding how dental negligence claims work, how compensation is calculated, and how to respond to a letter of claim is critical for protecting your registration, finances and reputation.

  • Dental negligence claims process
  • Compensation amounts and settlements
  • Implant, cosmetic and orthodontic claims
  • Informed consent and record keeping
  • Limitation periods and patient complaints

GDC Investigations & Fitness to Practise

A General Dental Council (GDC) investigation can be career-defining. Allegations may arise from patient complaints, employer referrals, criminal convictions, clinical concerns or professional conduct issues.

Early legal advice is essential when responding to GDC correspondence, drafting reflective statements and preparing for interim orders or fitness to practise hearings.

  • GDC complaint investigations
  • Fitness to practise hearings
  • Interim suspension and conditions
  • Professional conduct allegations
  • Appeals and restoration

Dental Indemnity & Professional Insurance

Dental indemnity insurance is a legal and professional necessity. Choosing the correct level of indemnity cover protects against negligence claims, GDC proceedings, legal costs and compensation payouts.

  • Occurrence-based vs claims-made cover
  • Indemnity limits and exclusions
  • Run-off cover and retirement protection
  • Vicarious liability for practice owners
  • Insurance disputes and policy reviews

Dental Employment Law & Practice Contracts

Employment law issues frequently arise within dental practices, including associate agreements, hygienist contracts, restrictive covenants, IR35 considerations and disputes over pay, notice and termination.

  • Associate dentist contracts
  • Self-employed vs employed status
  • Restrictive covenants and non-compete clauses
  • Practice sale and purchase agreements
  • Shareholder and partnership disputes

Regulatory Compliance & Risk Management

Regulatory compliance failures can lead to enforcement action, fines, CQC intervention and reputational damage. Proactive legal risk management helps prevent issues before they arise.

  • CQC compliance and inspections
  • Data protection and GDPR
  • Consent policies and documentation
  • Clinical governance systems
  • Complaint handling procedures
