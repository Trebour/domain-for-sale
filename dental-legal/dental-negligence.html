Dental Negligence Claims & Compensation Guidance

Expert guidance for dentists, patients, and practice owners on handling dental negligence claims, understanding compensation, and navigating legal challenges in the UK dental industry.

What is Dental Negligence?

Dental negligence occurs when a dentist or dental practice breaches their professional duty, resulting in harm to a patient. Claims may arise due to misdiagnosis, poor treatment outcomes, failure to obtain informed consent, or lack of proper care standards.

Common Types of Dental Negligence Claims

  • Failed or incorrect treatment (fillings, crowns, implants)
  • Misdiagnosis or delayed diagnosis
  • Oral surgery complications
  • Orthodontic errors or ineffective treatment
  • Failure to follow hygiene and safety standards

Dental Negligence Compensation

Compensation depends on the severity of harm, financial losses, pain, and suffering. Claims may cover:

  • Medical costs and corrective treatment
  • Lost income due to inability to work
  • Pain, suffering, and distress
  • Future care and ongoing treatment
