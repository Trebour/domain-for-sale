Responding to Patient Complaints: Guidance for UK Dentists

Patient complaints are a common part of modern dentistry. Knowing how to respond correctly can reduce legal risk, prevent escalation to the GDC, and protect your professional reputation.

Why Patient Complaints Matter

Patient complaints are often the first warning sign of wider legal or regulatory risk. If handled poorly, a complaint can escalate into a dental negligence claim or a GDC investigation.

Common Causes of Dental Complaints

Most complaints relate to communication rather than clinical care. Common issues include consent, fees, treatment outcomes, delays, and perceived attitude.

How to Respond to a Patient Complaint

A calm, professional, and timely response is essential. Dentists should acknowledge complaints promptly, investigate thoroughly, and respond clearly in writing.

Complaints and GDC Risk

Complaints that are dismissed or handled defensively are more likely to be referred to the General Dental Council. Clear documentation and appropriate apologies can reduce escalation.

Learning From Complaints

Complaints should be used as learning opportunities. Reviewing procedures, improving communication, and updating training can reduce future risk.
