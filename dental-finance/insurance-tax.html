Professional Indemnity

Professional indemnity protects dentists from claims arising from clinical negligence, advice, or other practice-related activities. Selecting the right policy involves evaluating coverage limits, exclusions, and whether associates and staff are included. Considerations include:

  • Policy limits and coverage thresholds
  • Inclusion of associates and practice staff
  • Claims handling procedures and access to legal support
  • Overlap with GDC investigations and regulatory compliance

For detailed guidance, see Dental Legal Hub and Dental Indemnity.

Practice Insurance

Practice insurance safeguards physical and operational assets, addressing unique dental risks such as equipment failure, business interruption, and property damage. Key considerations include:

  • Buildings and contents insurance for clinic premises
  • Business interruption cover to mitigate income loss during downtime
  • Equipment and technology protection, including CAD/CAM systems and imaging devices
  • Compliance with lender requirements for financed equipment

Further guidance on protecting equipment and technology investments is available in Dental Equipment & Services and Practice Loans.

Tax Planning for Dentists

Effective tax planning helps dentists retain more profit while staying fully compliant. Strategies include:

  • Choosing the optimal business structure (sole trader, partnership, or limited company)
  • Claiming all allowable business expenses
  • Timing income and expenses for optimal tax efficiency
  • Pension contributions and long-term financial planning

Integrating tax planning with Business Finance strategies, equipment investment (Dental Equipment Finance), and loan planning (Practice Loans) ensures financial efficiency across the practice.

Risk Management & Regulatory Compliance

Insurance and tax decisions are inseparable from legal and regulatory obligations. Compliance with GDC, CQC, and employment regulations protects both the practice and staff. Key actions include:

  • Maintaining accurate records of insurance coverage and contracts
  • Ensuring all associates and staff are covered under appropriate policies
  • Documenting compliance procedures for audits or inspections
  • Reviewing loan and lease agreements to avoid conflicts with insurance or indemnity arrangements

Comprehensive legal compliance guidance is available at Regulatory Compliance.
